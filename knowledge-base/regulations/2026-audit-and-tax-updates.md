# 2026 Audit & Tax Regulatory Updates

Quick reference for the authorities that drive several skills added in 2026. Pinpoint to the underlying standards or code when drafting output — do not rely on this summary alone.

## Tax: R&D Expensing & Research Credit (OBBBA 2025)

- **One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 (OBBBA)**, Pub. L. 119-21, signed July 4, 2025.
- **New IRC §174A** restores **full current-year deduction** for domestic research or experimental (R&E) expenditures paid or incurred in tax years beginning **after December 31, 2024**. Foreign R&E remains under §174 with **15-year** amortization.
- **Small-business retroactive election** — Taxpayers whose average annual gross receipts for the three prior tax years do not exceed **$31M** (measured at the controlled-group level) may retroactively apply §174A expensing to **2022, 2023, and 2024**. Election deadline: **July 6, 2026**. Path is via amended return or, in some fact patterns, an automatic method change under Form 3115.
- **Transition of unamortized §174 basis** — Taxpayers with amounts capitalized under §174 from 2022–2024 that are not addressed by the small-business retroactive election recover those amounts under the OBBBA transition rule over the remaining amortization period or as elected.
- **§280C(c) addback** — If the §41 research credit is claimed without a §280C(c) reduced-credit election, QREs deducted under §174A must be added back to taxable income by the amount of the credit.
- **State conformity** — Not every state conforms to §174A on the federal schedule; check each jurisdiction's conformity date.

## Tax: Form 6765 Section G (New Disclosure)

- **Form 6765** (Credit for Increasing Research Activities), **Instructions Rev. December 2025**.
- **Section G** requires **business-component-level** reporting of QREs, with wages split into three buckets: (a) direct research, (b) direct supervision, (c) direct support.
- **Mandatory for tax years beginning after December 31, 2025** on originally filed returns, except for:
  - Qualified Small Businesses (QSBs) electing the payroll-tax offset under §41(h); or
  - Taxpayers with QREs **≤ $1.5M** and gross receipts **≤ $50M** at the controlled-group level.
- **Required per business component**: identifier/name, type (product / process / software / technique / formula / invention), "information sought to be discovered," QREs by the three wage categories plus supplies, contract research (at 65% or 100%), and computer-rental/leased cloud costs.
- **Practitioner implication**: many taxpayers track wages only at the department level; Section G now expects project-level detail. Expect scope expansion on R&D-credit engagements.

## Tax: Sales Tax Nexus Post-Wayfair

- **South Dakota v. Wayfair, Inc.**, 585 U.S. ___ (2018) established **economic nexus** as a valid basis for state sales-tax collection obligations — physical presence is no longer required.
- Most states use a **$100,000 in sales** threshold; some (e.g., Texas) use **$500,000**; a number of states also apply **transaction-count** thresholds (e.g., 200 transactions).
- Marketplace-facilitator laws shift collection to platforms for many sellers.
- Compliance obligations include registration, collection, remittance, filing, and exemption-certificate management in each nexus state.

## Tax: ASC 740 Pillar Two

- **OECD Pillar Two** Global Anti-Base Erosion (GloBE) rules impose a 15% minimum effective tax rate on multinational groups with annual consolidated revenues ≥ €750M.
- GloBE top-up taxes are treated as income taxes under **ASC 740**; calculation is based on financial-statement net income with specified modifications.
- Effective dates vary by jurisdiction; many jurisdictions brought Income Inclusion Rule (IIR) and Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT) into force for tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2024.

## Audit: Fraud Standards

- **AU-C 240** — U.S. GAAS: *Consideration of Fraud in a Financial Statement Audit*. Key paragraphs:
  - **.15** — engagement-team discussion requirement
  - **.27** — rebuttable presumption that revenue recognition is a fraud risk
  - **.30** — element of unpredictability in audit procedures
  - **.32** — mandatory response to management override: journal-entry testing, estimate retrospective, review of significant unusual transactions
  - **.42–.44** — communication to those charged with governance and documentation
- **PCAOB AS 2401** — Consideration of Fraud in a Financial Statement Audit (issuers).
- **ISA 240 (Revised 2024)** — IAASB; effective for audits of financial statements for periods **beginning on or after December 15, 2026**. Key changes: "fraud lens" integrated across all ISAs; new application guidance on **entity use of technology to facilitate fraud** and **auditor use of automated tools** for fraud risk assessment and testing; strengthened requirements on professional skepticism and fraud-risk communications.

## Audit: Going Concern Standards

- **ASC 205-40** — U.S. GAAP, management's going-concern assessment. Key guidance:
  - **-50-4** — management's assessment requirement
  - **-50-5** — examples of conditions and events
  - **-50-12** — disclosure when substantial doubt is alleviated by management's plans
  - **-50-13** — disclosure when substantial doubt is not alleviated
- **ASC 205-30** — Liquidation-basis accounting, required when liquidation is imminent.
- **AU-C 570** — U.S. GAAS auditor's evaluation. Key paragraphs:
  - **.10** — evaluate management's assessment
  - **.13–.17** — audit procedures
  - **.22–.23** — separate section in the auditor's report when substantial doubt exists
- **PCAOB AS 2415** — Consideration of an Entity's Ability to Continue as a Going Concern (issuers).
- **ISA 570 (Revised 2024)** — IAASB; effective for audits of financial statements for periods **beginning on or after December 15, 2026**. Key change: auditor is required to design and perform procedures to evaluate management's assessment **irrespective of whether events or conditions have been identified**.

## Lease Accounting Reference

- **ASC 842** — lease accounting standard effective for all non-public entities for fiscal years beginning after December 15, 2021. Requires recognition of ROU assets and lease liabilities for leases > 12 months.
- Disclosure requirements under **ASC 842-20-50**: lease cost breakdown (operating / finance / short-term / variable), maturity analysis, balance-sheet classification (current vs. non-current), weighted-average remaining lease term, weighted-average discount rate, cash paid for operating leases.
